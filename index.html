High Blood Sugar Levels

Chronically high blood sugar levels (hyperglycemia) are a hallmark of uncontrolled diabetes. They can lead to symptoms such as frequent urination, increased thirst, and fatigue.

Restrictive Diets

Many individuals with diabetes are encouraged to follow restrictive diets that limit carbs, sugars, and processed foods. While these diets are effective in controlling blood sugar, they can feel overwhelming or unsustainable.

Diabetes Complications

If not well-managed, diabetes can lead to a range of complications: Neuropathy: Nerve damage causing numbness or pain, often in the feet or hands. Retinopathy: Eye damage that can lead to vision loss.

Fatigue and Low Energy

People with diabetes often experience fatigue and low energy, partly due to fluctuating blood sugar levels. When glucose (the body's primary energy source) doesn't reach the cells effectively.

Obesity

Obesity is a significant risk factor for Type 2 Diabetes because excess fat, especially around the abdomen, can increase insulin resistance. A focus on sustainable weight loss through lifestyle changes, rather than crash diets, is key.
